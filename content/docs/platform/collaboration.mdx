Modern automation rarely stays with a single author. Teams co-build workflows, hand off maintenance, or pair an
**editor** with an **operator** who only executes. Gapflow supports **in-organization collaboration** without
opening your tenant to the entire internet: sharing is between **members of the same organization**, mediated by
**permissions** and explicit **share** records (workspace-level, workflow-level, view vs edit semantics).

Confusion arises when people equate **admin visibility**—seeing that a workspace exists—with **collaboration
rights**. Governance visibility helps support; **sharing** grants day-to-day access. This page focuses on the
latter pattern and best practices for least privilege.

{/* MANUAL-CONTENT-END */}

## Collaboration boundaries

All collaboration scenarios discussed here assume:

- Both parties are **members** of the **same organization** (or will be after invite acceptance).  
- The organization is **active** and within **plan limits** for members and workflows.  
- Appropriate **capabilities** exist after sharing—sharing alone may not bypass org-wide bans on execution.  

If someone is “outside” your org entirely, you cannot share a workspace to them without a **cross-org** feature
your deployment may not offer—confirm with operators.

## Workspace sharing

**Workspace sharing** grants another user access to **everything** (or a defined subset) inside a workspace—often the
right choice when a squad co-owns a fleet of workflows.

Typical capabilities include:

- **View** — browse workflow list, maybe open read-only canvas  
- **Edit** — modify workflows, depending on granular policy  
- **Manage members** — invite/remove collaborators on that workspace—restricted to trusted leads  

<Tabs items={['When to use workspace share', 'When to avoid it']}>
  <Tab>
    Use workspace-level sharing for **long-lived teams** where most workflows are collectively maintained—think
    “Marketing ops” or “Support automations.”
  </Tab>
  <Tab>
    Avoid workspace-wide share when you only need a **single** sensitive workflow exposed—prefer **workflow-level**
    or clone a minimal template into an isolated workspace.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Workflow sharing

**Workflow sharing** targets **individual canvases**—ideal for contractor access, reviews, or peer programming on
one automation without exposing neighboring intellectual property.

Shares record:

- **Who** granted access  
- **With whom**  
- **Permission** level (e.g., view vs edit)  

Exact UX labels depend on the build, but the intent is consistent: **narrow blast radius**.

<Callout type="info">
  Even with edit rights, **organization credentials** still gate tool execution—collaborators cannot magically use
  secrets they are not permitted to reference.
</Callout>

## Invitation workflow

Most collaborators must **join** the org before sharing records apply:

<Steps>
  <Step>Admin or member with invite rights sends an <strong>organization invitation</strong>.</Step>
  <Step>Invitee accepts and becomes a <strong>member</strong>.</Step>
  <Step>Assign <strong>baseline permissions</strong> so they can enter routes—see <a href="./roles-permissions">Roles &amp; permissions</a>.</Step>
  <Step>Use <strong>Share</strong> on workspace or workflow to grant resource-specific access beyond baseline.</Step>
  <Step>Confirm the collaborator can <strong>save</strong> or <strong>execute</strong> as intended in a test workflow.</Step>
</Steps>

## Versus admin oversight

**Organization admins** may have **visibility** into member workspaces for auditing. That does **not** replace
collaboration shares: visibility helps answer “does this workspace exist?” while **sharing** answers “may Alice edit
this canvas with Bob?”

## Best practices

- Prefer **least privilege**: start view-only, elevate to edit only after training.  
- **Re-share** when team membership rotates—old shares may linger if offboarding is manual.  
- Pair collaboration with [Resources & plans](./resources-plans)—adding members or workflows may hit caps.  
- Document **ownership**: name a primary maintainer per critical workflow to avoid orphaned graphs.  

## Troubleshooting collaboration issues

| Symptom | Likely fix |
| --- | --- |
| User sees workspace but canvas is read-only | Grant **edit** share or capability |
| User lacks credentials to run tools | Connect org credential or grant permission to use it |
| Share button missing | User lacks **share** capability on resource |
| Invitee never appears in members list | Invitation still pending or wrong email domain policy |
