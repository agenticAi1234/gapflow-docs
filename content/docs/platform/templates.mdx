**Templates** bridge personal authoring and **organizational reuse**. A template is a packaged workflow—blocks,
connections, loop structure, and tool configuration—plus **marketing-style metadata** (title, services/categories,
short description) that helps others discover it in **Browse templates**. Because templates can encode best
practices and approved integration patterns, many companies treat them as **governance artifacts**: only reviewed
templates should appear in the public library.

Publishing is **not** automatically the same as “share this JSON with a friend.” Depending on policy, submissions
may sit in **pending review** until a **super admin** approves them. That gate protects your brand and prevents
unfinished experiments from appearing next to production-ready starters.

{/* MANUAL-CONTENT-END */}

## Overview of the template lifecycle

At a high level:

1. A builder **authors** a workflow to the point it is reproducible.  
2. They **publish** (or submit) through the templates UI—supplying metadata and confirming snapshot capture.  
3. A **reviewer** (often super admin) **approves** or **rejects**; optional remarks explain decisions.  
4. **Approved** templates surface in **Browse templates** with filters (search, **service** categories).  
5. Consumers click **Use template** to **instantiate** a new workflow in their workspace (subject to permissions).  

<Callout type="info">
  The **snapshot** is the frozen technical graph; **metadata** is what humans read on cards. Editing metadata after
  approval may be allowed for operators without changing historical snapshot semantics—see your deployment.
</Callout>

## My templates vs browse templates

| Area | Audience | Typical contents |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **My templates** | The publisher | Drafts, **pending review**, **approved**, **rejected** items; may show reviewer remarks |
| **Browse templates** | Whole org or deployment | **Approved** only; discovery-focused listing |

Use **My templates** to track **submission state** and read feedback. Use **Browse** when you want a **curated**
starting point you trust.

## Publishing: what gets captured

Publishing usually records:

- **Workflow snapshot** — nodes, edges, loops, sub-block values needed to recreate behavior  
- **Identity** — who published, from which organization context  
- **Metadata** — `templateName`, use case / summary, description, **categories** (services), sometimes tags  
- **Optional AI assistance** — some deployments pre-fill descriptions from LLMs; availability varies  

<Tabs items={['Categories / services', 'Permissions']}>
  <Tab>
    **Categories** align workflows with filters in Browse (for example “Customer support”, “Marketing”). Consistent
    tagging improves search relevance. Coordinators should maintain a **controlled vocabulary** if you have many
    publishers.
  </Tab>
  <Tab>
    **Publishing**, **listing mine**, and **instantiating** templates are permission-gated. If a user cannot see
    **Use template**, check org capabilities—not every member may clone library content in restricted environments.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Review and quality bar

Before submitting for review, authors should:

- **Run** the workflow in a test workspace with safe data.  
- **Remove** personal secrets from sub-blocks—prefer org credentials references.  
- **Document** required credentials in the description so consumers know prerequisites.  
- **Verify** loop bounds and external calls will not accidentally spam production systems when cloned.  

Reviewers should reject with **actionable remarks** so authors can iterate quickly.

## Consuming a template

<Steps>
  <Step>Open <strong>Browse templates</strong> and filter/search by name, id, or service.</Step>
  <Step>Preview metadata and (if available) workflow snapshot or diagrams.</Step>
  <Step>Click <strong>Use template</strong> and select target <strong>workspace</strong> if prompted.</Step>
  <Step>Reconnect or authorize credentials required by tool blocks—imports do not magically carry secrets.</Step>
  <Step><strong>Save</strong> and test before promoting to production traffic.</Step>
</Steps>

## AI-assisted metadata (where enabled)

Some builds offer helpers to **draft** descriptions from the workflow graph. Treat suggestions as **starting
text**: humans should still edit for clarity, compliance, and accurate prerequisite lists.

## Troubleshooting

| Issue | Likely cause |
| --- | --- |
| Stuck in **pending** | Awaiting super admin review |
| **Rejected** with blank remark | Policy mismatch—ask reviewer for detail |
| Browse shows **0** after picking service | Filter mismatch or API issue—verify category spelling |
| **Use template** fails | Missing instantiate permission or workspace selection |
