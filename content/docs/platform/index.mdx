Gapflow is built around **organizations**: your workflows, workspaces, folders, usage metrics, and a large part of product settings are **scoped to the active organization**. When you switch organizations, you are effectively switching **context**—which credentials apply, which members you collaborate with, and which resource limits govern what you can create or run.

This **Platform & administration** section is for everyone who needs to understand **how the product is governed**, not only how to drag blocks on a canvas. It connects the dots between:

- **Who** can do what (roles, invitations, granular permissions)
- **How much** the organization can consume (Basic, Enterprise, Custom plans)
- **How** workflows are saved, versioned, imported, and exported
- **How** templates enter the public library (including super admin review)
- **Where** execution history lives versus **definition** history (change logs)
- **How** to connect tools and AI providers (Settings, environment variables)
- **How** peers share workspaces and workflows inside the same tenant

If you are onboarding a new team, read [Organization model](./organization) and [Roles & permissions](./roles-permissions) first so you are not surprised when a new hire “has an account but cannot run anything” until permissions are assigned.

## How this section relates to the rest of the docs

The sidebar still groups **Blocks**, **Tools**, **Connections**, and **Execution** under **Create** and **Execution**. Those chapters explain **authoring** workflows. **Platform & administration** explains **who may author**, **in which org**, **under which limits**, and **how operational data (logs, reports, templates)** is surfaced.

You do not need to read everything here before your first workflow—but returning here after you understand blocks and tools will clarify **why** some buttons are disabled, **why** imports fail policy checks, or **why** templates stay in “pending review.”

## Blocks, tools, and execution (quick pointer)

- **Blocks** and **tools** remain documented under **Create** in the sidebar.
- **Connections** explain how data flows between blocks at runtime.
- **Execution** and **variables** document engine behavior that applies **within** whatever org you have selected.

If you are migrating from an older **autosave-only** workflow, treat [Workflow editor](./workflow-editor) as required reading: saves are now an intentional checkpoint, and version history is a first-class feature.

## Support and customization

Deployments may **rename** menu items (for example “Tool integration” vs “Credentials”) or **hide** super admin routes entirely. When in doubt, match the **concept** in this docs section to the closest label in your UI—or ask your platform operator which capabilities are enabled for your tenant.
